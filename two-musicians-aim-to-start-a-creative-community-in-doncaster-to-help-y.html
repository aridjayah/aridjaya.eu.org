Two musicians aim to start a creative community in Doncaster to help youths who want to pursue the arts

25 Aug 2020 by Abrega Slona

A musical duo have used their spare time during the lockdown to write and record their very first rap tracks and want to help other aspiring artists break into the industry.
The lockdown has provided young musicians Lewis Russell and David Walusimbi with the opportunity to work on releasing their first project entitled ‘GSD’ short for Get Stuff Done.
The seven track collection is a combination of rap and pop music all written, produced and sung by the duo.
Lewis, aged 21, from Balby, said: “We are hoping to start a creative collective in Doncaster under the name GSD.
